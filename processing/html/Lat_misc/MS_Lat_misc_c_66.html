History

Origin:
15th century, late - 16th century, early
English

Provenance and Acquisition: Humfrey Newton (1466-1536) (on whom see Earwaker, 1877, I, 121-2). The descendants of H. Newton, the Wards and the Bromley-Davenports, first at Pownall Hall, and later at Capesthorne (see Horwood, 1874, 80). Lent in the mid-17th century to John Booth , the Cheshire antiquary, who suffered it 'to be destroyed with moisture and eaten with ratts' (fol. vi recto). Sold at Sotheby's, 27 October 1947, lot 259; bought by the Bodleian.
