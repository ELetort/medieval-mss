History

Origin:
15th century, third quarter
English

Provenance and Acquisition: Philip, Landgrave of Hesse (said to have been previously owned by his uncle, Prince Albert) Given by him in 1692 to Daniel Duncan (1649-1735), the Scottish Doctor (see DNB) Thomas Rawlinson, F.R.S., for whom the present binding was executed, his sale, 4 March 1733/34, lot 531 D. F. Henley Welch, sold at Sotheby's 31 March, 1952, lot 23 (with a cutting from the sale catalogue glued to fol. ir), bought by Halliday for £60. Sold at Sotheby's, 10 Dec. 1969, lot 40, bought by Quaritch on behalf of the Bodleian
