Physical Description

  • Secundo Folio: Mt vj factum
  • Format: codex
    • Support: Material: chart

      Material Support: paper, quire-centres strengthened with parchment

      Extent: i + 415 + i Dimensions (leaf): 313 × 215 mm.
      Dimensions (written): 235 × 140 mm.

      Collation: 110 wants 10 (blank ?) 2–2612 2712 wants 10, 11, probably blank, after f. 318 28–3512. Catchwords cropped away at the start and the end.

    • Layout:

      Columns: 2
      Written Lines: 40 50
      Frame ruling. 2 cols. 40–50 lines.

  • Hand(s):

    Written in cursiva, by several hands, changing at ff. 118 (111), 316, 320, and 404, and for almost all f. 359v and for 13 lines on f. 381vb.

  • Decoration:

    Red initials, most spaces left blank after f. 304.

  • Binding:

    Contemporary binding of wooden boards covered with leather, bearing five stamps, a round eagle displayed (20 mm. diam.), a foliage-work diamond (28 x 19 mm.), two ivy-leaves on a stem (30 x 14 mm.), and a seated figure holding up a ring (8 mm. square), the first occupying the full diamonds and the second the half-diamonds at the sides of the centre section, which is surrounded by a border with the third stamp along the sides and the fourth in the corners, the central lattice and the borders being defined by four fillets with the central band hatched, with the fifth stamp, ‘yhs’, (28 x 21 mm.), in the centre of the top border on the front; metal corner-pieces; five bosses on each cover, all missing; two clasps, missing; rebacked.

  • Accompanying Material: Quire-centres strengthened with parchment strips from a noted service-book, s. xii.

History

Origin:
1462
German

Provenance and Acquisition: Perhaps written in part by the named ‘compilator’, epistoler/gospeller (?) at ‘Smoll’, see art. 13, subsequently a Carthusian at Erfurt: ‘Volumen hoc pertinet ad domum saluatoris ordinis carthus’ prope erford' a domino iodoco c(er)sten anno nouiciatus sui 1465 eidem intestatus traditus', inside front cover, see MBDS, p. 498 O.49. Lot 126 in Bülow sale. ‘G. Sumner Woodmansey 1856. Case B AA’, f. 1; lot 12 in Sumner sale. Relevant cutting from an English bookseller's catalogue at the end. Snowshill Manor , deposited in the Bodleian by the National Trust in 1981.
