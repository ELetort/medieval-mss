Summary

Summary: Lectionaries and Anastasius Sinaita A 9th cent., B 8th cent. (?), C 13th cent.

Physical Description

  • Palimpsest: lower script from two manuscripts (fols. 1-99, 100-130), combined to take the upper script.
  • Format: codex
    • Support: Material: perg

      Material Support: parchment

      Extent: Dimensions (leaf): 310 × 240 mm.

      Foliation: Foliated 1–11, 12ab, 13–130.

      Collation: first quire missing, (fol. 1) ii–xiii 8, (fol. 96) xiv 4, (fol. 100) xv–xvii 8, (fol. 124) xviii 8 (8 canc.). Some quires numbered on first recto above the left-hand column.

  • Binding:

    Binding East European, 15th–16th cent., blind-stamped brown leather with hunting-scene stamps.

    Physical Description

    • Format: codex
      • Support: Material: perg

        Material Support: parchment

        Extent: Dimensions (ruled): 230 × 170 mm.

      • Layout:

        Columns: 2
        Ruling: 2 columns of 21–2 lines, 12–14 letters in each line. Ruling type Leroy 20C2. The sheets were originally a little longer, as the liturgical direction on fol. 15v has been partially cut away.

    • Hand(s): One hand; the letters slope slightly to the left and are of uneven width (epsilon, omicron and sigma being rather narrow). Apparently a typical late uncial. Traces of ecphonetic notation visible.
    • Decoration:

      Much decoration: apart from the use of vermilion in some titles and headings there are πύλαι on fols. 66v and 93r, other headpieces and numerous initials in more than one colour; many of the latter are curiously tall (e.g. tau on fols. 18r and 91v, which is rather like the example shown in plate 429 of K. Weitzmann, Die byzantinische Buchmalerei …, Berlin 1935, from MS. Jerusalem, St. Saba 2, 10th cent. minuscule).

      Ornamental headpieces and decorated initials at the beginnings of lections.(Hutter iii. 13)

    Physical Description

    • Format: codex
      • Support: Material: perg

        Material Support: parchment

        Extent: Dimensions (ruled): 240 × 180 mm.

      • Layout:

        Columns: 2
        Ruling: 2 columns of 22–3 lines, 9–11 letters to each line. Ruling type Leroy 00A2. The sheets were originally larger, as the liturgical directions on the top margin of fols. 101v, 102v, 104v have been cut away. A cross on the top left corner of fol. 124r appears to be a quire signature.

    • Hand(s): One hand, bold large letters, upright; it gives the impression of being earlier than the ninth century.
    • Decoration:

      Rubrication: ornamentation consisting of large initials in very simple style. No ecphonetic notation visible.

      Ornamental strips and decorated initials at the beginnings of lections. (Hutter iii. 13)

    Physical Description

    • Format: codex
      • Support: Material: perg

        Material Support: parchment

        Extent: Dimensions (ruled): 230 × 170 mm.

      • Layout:

        Columns: 2
        Ruling: ruling types taken over from the uncial books. Two columns of 29–37 lines.

    • Hand(s): One scribe.
    • Decoration:

      Rubrication: vermilion used for titles, initials, headings in simple penwork ornament, marginal indications of contents and occasional scholia.

      Ornamental headpieces, strips and decorated initials. (Hutter iii. 13)

    • Additions:

      Prayers, 13th cent., on fols. 105v, 105r.

      (fol. 130r) τὸ καλανδολόγιον, (fol. 130rv) selenodromion, probably 13th or 14th cent.; see S. Weinstock, Catalogus codicum astrologorum graecorum, vol. ix, part I, Codices Oxonienses, Bruxelles 1950, p. 22.
