Summary

Summary: Gregorius Nazianzenus, Menologion Palimpsest: A 13th cent., B 11th cent.

Physical Description

  • Composite: A, B
  • Additions: fols. 164v–165v, 13th or early 14th cent., Homerus Α 1–120, symbol O1 in T.W. Allen, Homeri Ilias i, Oxford 1931, p. 29. fol. 166v, 14th cent., various etymological notes, beg. ἀμφίβληϲτρον ϲημαίνει τὸ δίκτυον παρὰ τὸ βάλλω. fol. 166v, 16th cent. note headed περὶ τοῦ κατενεγκεῖν φλέγμα ἐκ τῆϲ κεφαλῆϲ.
  • Binding:

    Binding of blind-stamped brown leather over wood, in late Byzantine style, 15th–16th cent., with hunting-scene stamps.

        Material Support: parchment

        Extent: i + 167 leaves. Dimensions (leaf): 213 × 70 mm.

        Foliation: foliated (i), 1–166, (167).

        Collation: (fol. 1) i–xx 8, (fol. 161) xxi 8 (6, 8 canc.). Quires numbered on top and/or bottom right corner of first recto and bottom left or right of last verso.

      • Layout:

        Columns: 2 6
        Ruling: usually 2 columns (but 3 on fols. 134v–160r and 6 on fol. 160rv) of 35–40 lines. The ruling of the earlier book (B) was used again. Dimensions (ruled): 155 × 90–110 mm.
