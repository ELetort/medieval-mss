  • The Worcester Fragments,
  • Note:
    Fifteen leaves of polyphonic music recovered from bindings of MSS. Auct. F. inf. 1. 3 (SC 2747), Bodl. 862 (SC 2730), and Hatton 30 (SC 4076). Interspersed with photostats of other fragments in Worcester Cathedral Library MS. Add. 68 and Brit. Mus. Add. MS. 25031. The MS. is fully described with facsimiles and a concordance of new with old foliations by L. A. Dittmer, The Worcester Fragments, Musicological Studies and Documents 2, Amer. Inst. of Musicology, 1957, and Publications of Medieval Music Manuscripts, no.6, Inst. of Med. Music, New York, 1960. The following list aims to clarify the sometimes confusing relationship between the original leaves and the modern reprocuctions in the volume, and between the various foliations. In addition to the foliations mentioned in the literature, the reproductions of fragments at Worcester have another modern pencil foliation, now erased: this was perhaps used temporarily, before the volume was bound.

Physical Description

  • Format: codex
    • Support: Material: perg

      Material Support: parchment

      Extent: 15 leaves, with 25 photostats = vi + 40 leavesDimensions (leaf): 270 × 200 mm.
      Much mutilated by binders; dimensions variable.

      Foliation: Medieval foliation in red ink upper-case roman numerals survives in the earlier part of the volume; at least two modern pencil foliations are present throughout (see under Text).

      Collation: The leaves do not necessarily come from a single original volume; there are too many lacunae to reconstruct the collation with any confidence, but it seems that quires of about ten leaves were common in the section with medieval foliation: the first quire must have been of 10 leaves, since fols. VI-VII in the medieval foliation are a bifolium; the quire including medieval fols. LXXXII-CI was probably also of 10; and that containing LXXIX-LXXXVIII was presumably of 12 leaves.

    • Layout:

      Columns: 1
      8–10 staves of 5 red lines

  • Decoration:

    red and blue initials, a few with flourishing.

  • Binding:

    Bodleian guardbook, c.1925; repaired in 1964.
